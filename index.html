Research

MVI (Molecular Virology & Immunology Lab) is currently a project which I hope to start soon. We have two broad areas of inquiry under the Bioinformatics umbrella. The first is virus-host interactions. The second is the immune response in chronic diseases. To address these topics, we use a broad range of approaches bioinformatics, genetics and genomics.

Gene Expression

Gene expression can be studied under the RNA sequencing set of methods, which include Bulk & Single Cell RNAseq; and Bulk & Single Cell RRBisulfateSeq. We use these approaches not only to profile the gene expression of cells under certain conditions but to deconvolve cell types, such as B or T Cells, and recover viral or bacterial signatures left at the molecular levels.

Structural Biology

We study the behavior and role of protein structures through computational methods that model the intricated dynamics that rule how proteins interact in a solvent and each other. We've used this approach to improve in silico epitope detection to enhance the adaptive immune response against peptide antigens. Furthermore, we studied viral evolution and capsid assembly through reconstructing the ancestrality of current viruses.

Resources

Resources for both research and teaching.

Resources

RNAseq Introduction & Exercises (From LIDI) This course illustrates the basics of bulk-RNAseq analyses.

Experimental Design Course (From Blackmon Lab) This course is intended to provide a foundation in the proper design of scientific research projects in the field of biology. A wide range of biological experiments are covered, and each type of experiment is explored with an eye toward choosing the appropriate statistical technique for analysis. By the end of the course, successful students will be able to design biological studies that are statistically tractable and perform basic statistical analyses using the programming language R.

R for biologists seminar (From Blackmon Lab): R is currently an essential tool for most evolutionary biologists. This seminar includes tutorials and example datasets for many basic analyses in R that are commonly encountered in ecology and evolution. The seminar starts off with the most basic steps (installation) so no prior experience is needed to use these exercises.

Foundations of evolutionary biology (From Blackmon Lab): An understanding of the foundational ideas that the modern synthesis of evolutionary biology is built on is essential for asking intelligent questions and understanding how your research fits into a larger understanding of life. I offered a foundations of evolutionary biology seminar course for graduate students that provided a structured environment to discuss some of the most important papers in evolutionary biology. This website contains a schedule and PDF files for all of the readings in this course.
